Research

Abstract

My name is Sriharsha Kocherla, and my research interests lie in quantum algorithms and machine learning.

I am currently working at the Computational Physics lab at Georgia Tech, where I focus on developing quantum algorithms for modeling fluid flows. I've been in this position since September 2021.

Prior to this, I was involved in ML research and contributed to the development of a model that detects retinal junctions.

Overall, I'm passionate about developing new computational approaches and am excited to continue exploring these fields.

Conferences & Publications

Fully quantum algorithm for lattice Boltzmann methods with application to partial differential equations
arXiv (Manuscript) | May 14th, 2023
Fluid flow simulations marshal our most powerful computational resources. In many cases, even this is not enough. Quantum computers provide an opportunity to speedup traditional algorithms for flow simulations. We show that lattice-based mesoscale numerical methods can be executed as efficient quantum algorithms due to their statistical features. This approach revises a quantum algorithm for lattice gas automata to eliminate classical computations and measurements at every time step. For this, the algorithm approximates the qubit relative phases and subtracts them at the end of each time step, removing a need for repeated measurements and state preparation (encoding). Phases are evaluated using the iterative phase estimation algorithm and subtracted using single-qubit rotation phase gates. This method delays the need for measurement to the end of the computation, so the algorithm is better suited for modern quantum hardware. We also demonstrate how the checker-board deficiency that the D1Q2 scheme presents can be resolved using the D1Q3 scheme. The algorithm is validated by simulating two canonical PDEs: the diffusion and Burgersâ€™ equations on different quantum simulators. We find good agreement between quantum simulations and classical solutions of the presented algorithm.
F. E. Chrit, S. Kocherla, B. Gard, E. F. Dumitrescu, A. Alexeev, and S. H. Bryngelson
Quantum lattice algorithms for solving partial differential equations
TQC 2022 (Conference Abstract) | June 9th, 2022
Computational modeling hinges on solving partial differential equations (PDEs) that require numerical solutions. These PDEs can comprise trillions of degrees of freedom and are thus expensive to solve classically. Recent developments in mesoscale numerical methods and quantum hardware present an opportunity to solve such PDEs efficiently. We analyze two such mesoscale methods: Lattice Gas Automata (LGA) and Lattice Boltzmann Methods (LBM). Quantum variants of these algorithms are implemented and analyzed. We show that existing approaches fail due to reliance on measurement and classical computation. The nature of this weakness is identified and algorithms that avoid it are formulated.
F. E. Chrit, S. Kocherla, A. Adams, J. Young, A. Alexeev, and S. H. Bryngelson
TensorFlow Object Detection Model for Retinal Vascular Junctions Extraction
GCAT 2021 (Conference paper) | October 3rd, 2021
Retinal junctions are important landmarks that are useful in the diagnosis of retinal diseases. But as the blood vessel structure is very complex, identification of these junctions requires an accurate, robust algorithm. We proposed a two-stage deep learning TensorFlow Object Detection (TFOD) framework to detect retinal junctions very accurately. In the first stage of the proposed framework, the network is trained to propose possible junctions, and in the next stage, they are refined entirely to reduce false predictions. Finally, the junctions are classified as crossovers and bifurcations. The experimentation was carried out on both the DRIVE and IOSTAR datasets. The results obtained are qualitatively presented.
M. Shoaib, G. N. Boini, S. Kocherla, Y. S. K. Rebba, L. K. Pampana and M. S. Rayudu, "TensorFlow Object Detection Model for Retinal Vascular Junctions Extraction," 2021 2nd Global Conference for Advancement in Technology (GCAT), Bangalore, India, 2021, pp. 1-6, doi: 10.1109/GCAT52182.2021.9587882.
